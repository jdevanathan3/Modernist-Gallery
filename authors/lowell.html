Amy Lowell: In Writing

Amy Lowell wrote her first collection of poetry, A Dome of Many-Coloured Glass, in 1912, which quickly helped her rise to fame. She became the chief defendant of Imagism, a literary movement started by Ezra Pound, after Pound shifted to Vorticism, a different form of Imagism.

Lowell spent most of her life living together with another woman, Ada Dwyer Russell, and so celebrated female beauty in her poems. For example, in her poem Venus Transiens,published in 1919, Lowell compares female beauty to godlike beauty:

Tell me, was Venus more beautiful
Than you are
When she topped
The crinkled waves,
Drifting shoreward
On her plaited shell?

She herself draws an explicit comparison with the painting Venus by Botticelli in this work. Amy Lowell died shortly after in 1925.
